US Accident Severity Prediction

The project "US Traffic Accident Severity Prediction" aims to develop a predictive model for assessing the severity of traffic accidents across the U.S. from 2016 to 2023. The dataset includes approximately 7.7 million accident records from 49 states, highlighting the critical need for effective accident severity modeling to understand contributing factors and devise preventive strategies. The project also provides insights into the factors influencing insurance premium costs, which vary significantly by state due to differences in state laws, uninsured driver rates, and economic conditions. The U.S. Department of Transportation estimates the annual economic cost of crashes at $340 billion, emphasizing the importance of improving safety measures and reducing costs.

ML Driven Customer Segmentation for Brazilian E-Commerce

In this project, we leveraged a comprehensive dataset from the Brazilian e-commerce platform, Olist Store, which includes 100,000 orders from 2016 to 2018 across various marketplaces. The dataset provides a wealth of information, such as order status, pricing, payment methods, freight performance, customer locations, product attributes, and customer reviews. Our goal was to apply machine learning techniques to perform customer segmentation, a crucial aspect of marketing and business strategy. By analyzing these diverse data dimensions, we aimed to gain practical experience in data analysis and uncover actionable insights.

Interest Profiling based on TikTok reviews

The project aims to develop a system for profiling TikTok users by analyzing their app review texts to categorize them into interest groups like Entertainment Enthusiasts and Educational Seekers, ultimately enhancing personalized content recommendations and user engagement. Initially, the project faced challenges with feature extraction using TF-IDF, which lacked deep contextual understanding, and ineffective labeling through clustering and manual methods. Moving forward, the plan is to implement zero-shot classification with pre-trained models for more accurate user interest annotations and to utilize BERT embeddings for richer feature extraction, thereby improving the overall profiling accuracy and effectiveness.
